General Guidelines

  1. Only candidates for high school graduation of the current school year are qualified to take the University of Santo Tomas Entrance Test (USTET).
  2. The following applicants must secure clearance from the UST Office of the Registrar before submitting their Application and Grades Form:
    1. Passers of the Philippine Educational Placement Test (PEPT) and the Non-Formal Education Accreditation and Equivalency (NFEA&E)
    2. Applicants who are graduates of previous school years
    3. Candidates for graduation from foreign schools
      • Application Fee for Filipinos - PhP 1,000.00
      • Application Fee for Foreigners - PhP 1,500.00
    4. Foreigners graduating from local schools
      • Application Fee - PhP 1,000.00
      • Please bring original academic records, certification for passing PEPT/NFEA&E, and alien certificate of registration papers, or passport, when applicable.
  3. Only the School Registrar or Principal may certify the High School Grades Form.
  4. For validation, the school seal should be affixed to the High School Grades Form. In the absence of a school seal, a clear photocopy of Form 137A (Transcript of Records) may be attached to the document.
  5. Metro Manila applicants are strongly advised to deposit their non-refundable application fee (Php 500.00) in cash at any Metrobank branch. Payments will not be accepted at the Office for Admissions (OFAD).
  6. Applicants who wish to take the USTET in their provinces should submit the accomplished Application and Grades Form and payment to the nearest Testing Center.
  7. A downloadable test permit will only be issued to applicants three (3) working days after paying the application fee. You can download your test permit at the Freshmen Admission Account by using your reference number.
  8. To avoid problems in the release of your USTET results, please forward the complete set of documents to the Office for Admissions (OFAD) personally or through any courier service.
  9. Passing the USTET is not the sole basis for admission to some colleges. A second screening is required by the College of Nursing and the College of Tourism & Hospitality Management in the form of an interview, the Conservatory of Music requires an audition, the College of Architecture and the College of Fine Arts & Design require an artwork rendition.
  10. Please take note of the USTET schedules and their corresponding deadlines for the payment of the non-refundable application fee (Php 500.00) at any Metrobank branch and submission of documents (ONLY FOR METRO MANILA APPLICANTS):
    USTET Schedule Deadline for Payment and Submission of Documents
    March 26, 2015 January 14, 2015
    August 22, 2015 August 16, 2015
    November 4, 2015 October 21, 2015
  11. Applicants are advised to visit the OFAD website (ofad.ust.edu.ph) and Activity Log every Friday for any announcement.
  12. USTET Results will be released online on January 28, 2016 at ofad.ust.edu.ph.
